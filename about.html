Hey, I'm Ayobami.

I'm a developer based in Nigeria, documenting my journey through code. This is where I write about the things I learn, the projects I build, and the challenges I face along the way.

Six months ago, I made a decision that changed everything. I decided to teach myself how to code. No computer science degree. No bootcamp. Just me, my laptop, and an internet connection.

The first few weeks were brutal. I would stare at error messages for hours, wondering if I had made a terrible mistake. But something kept pulling me back to the keyboard. Every small victory, every bug I finally squashed, reminded me why I started.

I created Zenthos Insights because I needed a place to process what I was learning. Writing forces you to truly understand something. You cannot explain a concept you do not grasp yourself. So I started writing, and somewhere along the way, other people started reading.

What I Write About

This is not a tutorial factory. I write about real experiences. The late nights debugging code that should have worked. The satisfaction of shipping something you built from scratch. The humbling realization that there is always more to learn.

You will find posts about JavaScript and web development, yes. But also about the mental side of learning to code. The imposter syndrome. The comparison trap. The days when nothing makes sense and you question everything.

My hope is that if you are on a similar path, you will find something here that resonates. Something that makes you feel less alone in the struggle. Because the struggle is real, but so is the reward on the other side.

Why "Zenthos"?

Zenthos comes from the Greek word for "life force." It represents that spark of curiosity and determination that keeps you going when things get hard. Every developer has it. That thing that makes you stay up past midnight chasing a solution. That refusal to give up even when the problem seems impossible.

This blog is my way of honoring that energy. Both in myself and in everyone else who has chosen this path.

Let's Connect

I genuinely love hearing from readers. Whether you want to share your own journey, ask a question, or just say hello, my inbox is always open. You can find me on Twitter, GitHub, or LinkedIn.

If you have a story to share, this platform is open to you. Hit the Write button and contribute to the community. Every perspective matters.

Thanks for being here. Now go build something.
